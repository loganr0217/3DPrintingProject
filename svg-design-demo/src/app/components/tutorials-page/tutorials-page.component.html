The Design Interface (TDI)

The design team at LightScreen.art have produced a large collection of templates representing a number of genres and supporting a number of window dimensions. Many users will find a template that's perfect for their custom lightscreen in Step Three, the section entitled “Choose Your Design”.

Our designers build these templates from our collection of panels and size them to fit with most popular window dimensions. But with more than a hundred panels and with a large range of window dimensions, the number of potential templates is nearly limitless and it's impossible to anticipate every possible combination. To address this, LightScreen.art has created the Template Design Interface (TDI or “Teddy”) and has made it available to all registered users. With our TDI, discriminating users can use the same tool as our designers to create a template from our panel collection that will fit both your window and your style. This tutorial will outline how to use TDI.

To access TDI user's must first register by selecting Signup from our main menu at the upper right of our website and must be logged in. To start TDI, select Contact from the menu bar and click on it in the footer at the bottom of the page. This will display the full interface which consists of three main sections: Panel Insertion, Autofill and Panel Information and Template Layout Interface. The first two sections are intended for panel loading and coding into our system and are available only to panel, not template, designers. If you'd like to know more about becoming a panel designer, contact us at info@lightscreen.art.

To create your custom template you'll be using only the Template Layout Interface section. Under that headline and under the subhead "Choose a template and enter your template's panel layout (e.g. 2x4)” you'll find a horizontal box containing a number of panels from left to right, each with a number at the bottom left of the panel. These panels represent the underlying panelset, i.e. a collection of panels that are all similar in nature and designed to be used together in a template. Using the scroller at the bottom of the box you'll see our full collection of panels that represent our full collection of panelsets. Clicking on any one of the panels in the box will open up another box below it. In that box you'll see the panel you just clicked on as well as all other panels in that set— you may have to use the scrollbar to view the entire panel set. For example, when clicking on panel two in the upper box you'll see four panels in the lower box. When clicking on panel twenty-three, you'll see five and a half panels and when you scroll to the right you'll see at least fifteen more. These are the panels you'll have to choose from as you build your template.

Below the panel boxes, you'll see two more boxes, one entitled “Left to Right” and one entitled “Top to Bottom”. In the first box you'll enter the number of horizontal panels and in the second the number of vertical panels. The number of panels you'll enter in these boxes is dependent upon the dimensions you entered in Step Two. You can determine the number of panels required for your window by viewing the templates that appear in Step Three after you've completed Step Two. For example, if you enter 24 inches (600 mm) wide by 24 inches high in Step Two, you'll see displayed templates that are two panels across and two panels down in Step Three. In this example, you'd then enter 2 in the “Left to Right” box and 2 in the “Top to Bottom” box. Once you've entered these values, click on the “Update dims (2x6)” button directly below. You should now see text on the right of the upper panel box reading "Current Layout: (your number)x(your number) Current Panel Location: 0,0”. This indicates that you're now ready to begin building your template.

Templates are built across from the upper left corner. So click on the panel in the panel list that you'd like to be in the upper left of your template. Click on the panel you'd like to be to the immediate right of the first panel and continue doing so until you've reached the end of the first row. Continuing with the above example, the second panel you click will be at the upper right, the third at lower left and the fourth at lower right. Use the Undo button if you've made a mistake or would like to explore multiple template designs. Note as well that you can manipulate the orientation of panels: clicking the “Rotate Last Panel” button will rotate the panel in 90 degree increments clockwise. Clicking the “Mirror Last Panel” button will flip the panel horizontally. These two buttons may be used in tandem to achieve all possible panel orientations.

Pro Tip: Although our design team has grouped panels that will work together well in a template, it's possible to build your template from multiple panelsets. Clicking on any of the numbered panels in the upper box will display the full panel set in the lower box. Just toggle between multiple panelsets and select panels in the lower box to build your custom template.

When you're satisfied with your template, click the "Create Layout” button. A modal will pop up and you'll be asked to confirm that you want to save your template. Click OK to save or cancel to go back to your template. After clicking OK, another modal will display in a few moments reading “LightScreen.art says” with a number below it. If the number is 1 you've successfully saved your template, any number other than 1 indicates an error and you may then contact us for help. When the template is saved you'll now be able to access it in Step Three as you would any standard template. Note that the “Switch to Template Autofill” button is for features not yet supported and may be ignored.




Measuring

Here are some tips to ensure accurate measurements for your Lightscreen!

  • Measurements taken should refer to the actual glass of your window (the part to be covered) and should not include any part of the frame, sill, or encasement.

  • Windows with dividers should be measured as one total window - just make sure to get an accurate divider width.

  • From your window dimensions, the number of dividers and the divider width, we'll compute the dimensions of each panel to fit inside of the dividers. The computed panel dimensions will be displayed at the confirmation step, we strongly suggest that you check our panel dimensions against your actual measurements of the glass between your dividers and adjust your inputs if necessary.

  • Using a soft measuring tape like the type used for sewing can help you get a more precise measurement. If you have a rigid tape measurer like the type used for carpentry, you can get the most accurate measurement by bending the tape at a 90 degree angle and getting as close to the edge of the glass as possible.

  • If your measurements are more precise than a sixteenth of an inch or whole millimeters, round down. You'll have to use decimals for fractional inches - a conversion chart can be found here

  • For double hung windows, take measurements for each sash separately. Even if the frame is the same width, there's a good chance the glass panes could vary.

  • Finally, if you find yourself questioning the measurements taken, it doesn't hurt to start from the beginning. Round your measurements down when there is uncertainty. We here at LightScreen will produce your covering to the exact measurements you specify, and while there are workarounds to a slightly small covering, one that is too large will be difficult if not impossible to install.




Installation

Installing your lightscreen is easier than most other window coverings - there's no need for permanent holes, screws, nails or messy water as with window film.

  1. Begin by unpacking all of your panels and laying them out in the order you designed. Use this time to make sure everything looks right.

  2. Starting with a panel intended for one of the lower corners of your lightscreen, remove the backing from the gel tape strips on the four corners of the panel. Removing this backing is far easier when the right technique is employed. We've found the best way to do it is to use a small sturdy pointed object such as a safety pin. First, press down on the tape tab backing so that the gel tape bonds well with the plastic frame. Then insert the pin on one of the short sides of the tape tab just underneath the backing and as close to the top of the gel tape as possible. Insert the pointed object at least one third of the distance of the tape: it's OK if the point pierces the backing. Then remove the backing by slowly prying up the pointed object. As you do, the backing will separate enough from the gel tape that it may be pinched with your fingers and pulled off. If the gel tape separates from the frame during this process simply push it back down with your finger. The adhesive used is similar to the fugitive glue often used in direct mail. Unlike fugitive glue, our adhesive is resealable and may be touched with bare skin without impacting its performance.

  3. Line up your panel with the corresponding corner of your window. Take your time and ensure this panel is level and flush with the frame of your window, as any error at this step will accumulate as more panels are added.

  4. Push firmly for 10 seconds while taking care to not break the window glass. If your lightscreen panel shifts during this process, you may gently remove it and restart.

  5. Repeat this process with the next panel in the row, ensuring it is flush with both the frame and adjacent panels.

  6. When starting a new row, make sure the corners of your panels line up square with those below them. Avoid leaving gaps that are large enough to let in light!

  7. Repeat this process from bottom to top until your entire Lightscreen is installed.
